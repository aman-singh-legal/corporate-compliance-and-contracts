Corruption risks damage organizational trust, disrupt business continuity, increase legal exposure, and weaken sustainability commitments.  
An **Anti-Corruption Governance Framework** helps organizations prevent bribery, enforce ethical conduct, manage fraud risks, and align with global laws such as:

- **UK Bribery Act 2010**  
- **US FCPA (Foreign Corrupt Practices Act)**  
- **OECD Anti-Bribery Convention**  
- **UN Global Compact Principle 10**  
- **EU Whistleblower Protection Directive**  
- **ISO 37001 Anti-Bribery Management System**

This document outlines the governance structure, policies, controls, and processes required for an effective anti-corruption program.

# üìå 1. Purpose of the Framework

This Anti-Corruption Governance Framework establishes:

- A zero-tolerance policy toward bribery, fraud, kickbacks, facilitation payments, and unethical business behavior.  
- Standards for employee conduct and third-party interactions.  
- Procedures to detect, prevent, and respond to corruption risks.  
- Governance structures aligned with international compliance expectations.  
- Integration with ESG (Governance pillar), risk management, procurement, and internal audit functions.

# üèõÔ∏è 3. Governance Structure

## **3.1 Board of Directors**
- Sets tone at the top ("zero-tolerance")  
- Approves anti-corruption policy  
- Oversees program effectiveness  
- Reviews major corruption risks annually  

## **3.2 Compliance Officer / Ethics Officer**
- Owns anti-corruption program  
- Conducts training and awareness  
- Oversees investigations  
- Maintains risk registers  
- Reports findings to senior leadership  

## **3.3 Management**
- Implements policies in their departments  
- Ensures controls are followed  
- Conducts due diligence on vendors  
- Reports any suspicious activity  

## **3.4 Internal Audit**
- Independent assessment of controls  
- Red-flag monitoring  
- Risk-based audits  
- Follow-up on corrective actions  

---

# üß© 4. Anti-Corruption Policy Principles

## **4.1 Zero Tolerance**
No form of bribery, corruption, or unethical payments allowed.

## **4.2 No Facilitation Payments**
Even small unofficial payments are prohibited.

## **4.3 Gifts & Hospitality Rules**
- Only modest, infrequent, transparent  
- No gifts in exchange for favors  
- Tracking mechanism required  
- Pre-approval needed above threshold  

## **4.4 Conflicts of Interest**
Employees must disclose financial or personal interests that may influence decisions.

## **4.5 Political Contributions & Charitable Donations**
- Must be lawful, approved, transparent  
- Not to be used as disguised bribes  

## **4.6 Accurate Books & Records**
No falsified transactions or hidden accounts.

## **4.7 Whistleblower Protection**
Safe, anonymous reporting channels with no retaliation.

# üõ†Ô∏è 6. Internal Controls & Procedures

### **6.1 Due Diligence**
- Background checks on vendors  
- Beneficial ownership verification  
- Sanctions & watchlist screening  
- Risk-based enhanced due diligence  

### **6.2 Contractual Safeguards**
All agreements should include:
- Anti-corruption clause  
- Audit rights  
- Termination rights for violations  

### **6.3 Payment Controls**
- Segregation of duties  
- Approval workflows  
- No cash payments  
- Verification of invoices & services  

### **6.4 Gifts & Entertainment Register**
- Centralized log  
- Limits & approval workflows  

### **6.5 Incident Reporting & Investigation**
- Anonymous reporting line  
- Investigation protocol  
- Evidence preservation  
- Corrective action plan
